About

The University of Waterloo Amateur Radio Club is a club at the University of Waterloo for exploring the amateur radio hobby.

The club is open to all students of the University, for both those who are licensed and those who are not.

History

The amateur radio club at the University of Waterloo is one of the oldests clubs on campus.

What do we do?
  • Course for those interested in getting there license
  • Operate our radios (HF/Simplex/Repeater)
  • Learn new technologies with software-defined radios; including studying various transmission types, data protocols, etc.
  • Construct radio equipment and accessories

From time-to-time, UWARC holds open events (for example: movie night). These events are open to anyone, not just members of the club.

Anyone is Welcome

Anyone is welcome to attend our meetings; an amateur radio license is not required to participate in our activities. If you are interested in operating on the radio, you can do so under the supervision of a licensed member.
